<html><head></head><body><h2 id="reader-question">Reader Question</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>I was reading your blog post about migrating from WordPress to Gatsby and had a question about the necessary timeline we should expect. How long did it take you to make your full migration?</p>
</blockquote>
<p><em>If you are wondering what post is being referred to its <a href="migrating-from-wordpress-to-gatsby">Why and How I migrated my website from Wordpress to Gatsby?</a></em></p>
<h2 id="my-answer">My Answer</h2>
<p>The timeline for the migration depends on the contents of your site.</p>
<p>I started the move away from WordPress to Gatsby in August 2019 and switched over in November 2019. You might think that 3 months is a long time for migrating posts, and you would be correct.</p>
<p>But, it's important to note that I was spending just a few hours a week on this. It would have probably taken me a few hours/days to make the switch if I worked on it full-time.</p>
<p>I used a starter, so I had a base to start from. But, there were a few things I had to modify(which is why it took time) before I was ready to make the switch.</p>
<ul>
<li>getting it to play nicely with Typescript.</li>
<li>making sure all the internal links work.</li>
<li>building an image gallery component for the image-heavy posts.</li>
<li>hunting for the plugins not included in the starter and configuring them</li>
</ul>
<p>If you are thinking of migrating your site, I would suggest doing what I did.</p>
<ul>
<li>Start with a gatsby starter/theme as your base.</li>
<li>If the theme you have chosen doesn't have all the plugins, just add those plugins in.</li>
<li>Get an initial version of your site up and running using Netlify.</li>
</ul>
<p>Update the styling, components, plugin configurations, add automated scripts etc., as you see fit. This will probably be a continuous thing.</p>
