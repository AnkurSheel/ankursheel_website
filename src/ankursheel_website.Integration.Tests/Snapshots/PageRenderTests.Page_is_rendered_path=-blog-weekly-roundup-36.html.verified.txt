<p>Every week I come across some great articles. Here are some of the posts I feel you will enjoy reading as much as I did.</p>
<p><strong>Maxwell Forbes</strong> shares his thoughts on why we should <a href="https://maxwellforbes.com/posts/use-examples">use more examples</a> to make it easier for our audience to understand our ideas. He also gives a counterpoint to most of the common excuses that people have to write examples.</p>
<p>After being rejected in an interview at a startup, have you ever felt that it was a rejection of you as a person or of your skills? <strong>Charity Majors</strong> shares the <a href="https://charity.wtf/2019/10/18/the-real-11-reasons-i-dont-hire-you/">11 reasons she might not hire someone even though they might be awesome</a>.</p>
<p>When we focus only on one solution to the exclusion of others, we stop learning. We can miss a different approach that might have been better. <strong>Chelsea Troy</strong> goes over how <a href="https://chelseatroy.com/2020/09/01/only-one-right-way/">applying a single set of practices is prevalent in the tech industry</a> and what we can do to avoid it.</p>
<p>A Dev huddle is a forum for developers to discuss technical topics and make decisions regarding technology. But running an effective meeting is difficult.<strong>Mario Fernandez</strong> tells you <a href="https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/how-to-use-the-dev-huddle-to-get-your-developers-on-the-same-page/">how to run an effective Dev Huddle</a>. Some of the points that he made can be applied to other meetings as well.</p>
<p>Hiring good people is challenging for any company. These <a href="https://robertheaton.com/2014/04/21/9-ways-to-get-that-engineer/">tweaks to your hiring process</a> by <strong>Robert Heaton</strong> can make it more likely that the people you make offers to accept them.</p>
<p><strong>Prasanjit Singh</strong> shares a few <a href="https://css-tricks.com/ground-rules-for-web-animations/">ground rules for working with animations</a> which can help your site stand out rather than kill the experience for the user.</p>
<p>Hope you enjoy reading these articles as much as I did.</p>
