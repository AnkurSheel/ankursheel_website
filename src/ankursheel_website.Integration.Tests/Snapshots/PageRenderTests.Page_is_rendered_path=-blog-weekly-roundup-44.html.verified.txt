<h2 id="my-13-year-old-has-an-800-phone"><a href="https://www.thehappysaver.com/blog/my-13-year-old-has-an-800-dollar-phone">My 13-year-old has an $800 phone</a></h2>
<p>Buying a phone is expensive, especially if you are a 13-year-old who wants an iPhone. <strong>Ruth</strong> shares a story about how her daughter could contribute 50% to an $800 iPhone purchase in 2 months.</p>
<p><quote quote="It's been a journey of many tiny lessons, many brief conversations and when necessary a firm bottom line, to build up her understanding of how money works. Her knowledge is growing in incremental steps" author="Ruth"></quote></p>
<p>This article shows how we can teach our children the habit of saving while also teaching them it's OK to spend money they have budgeted.</p>
<h3 id="key-takeaways">Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li>Teach your child to save/invest a percentage of every dollar they earn.</li>
<li>For expensive purchases, agree to pay half the amount.</li>
<li>For massive targets, break them into smaller digestible chunks by using a "savings tracker".</li>
<li>Look at employment opportunities available to them, and if it needs the help of an adult, offer to help if they are willing to work.</li>
<li>For a kid, who have all their needs taken care of, it is the best time to teach them the habit of saving a percentage of their income.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://www.thehappysaver.com/blog/my-13-year-old-has-an-800-dollar-phone">Link to Article</a></p>
<h2 id="do-i-need-a-financial-advisor"><a href="https://www.iwillteachyoutoberich.com/blog/do-you-need-a-financial-advisor/">Do I Need a Financial Advisor</a></h2>
<p>How do we know if we need a financial advisor? <strong>Ramit Sethi</strong> explains the criteria for needing a financial advisor and why most people don't need one.</p>
<p><quote quote="Most people don't need a financial advisor. We have such simple needs that with a little bit of time we can get an automatic personal finance infrastructure working for us" author="Ramit Sethi"></quote></p>
<h3 id="key-takeaways-1">Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li>We need an advisor only if we have over $1 million in investable assets, have a complex financial situation, need a second set of eyes or a behavioural coach.</li>
<li>Get a fee-only advisor who is a fiduciary, meaning they are legally required to put our best interests first.</li>
<li>Reach out to at least 10 advisors and set up calls with 5.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://www.iwillteachyoutoberich.com/blog/do-you-need-a-financial-advisor/">Link to Article</a></p>
<h2 id="how-to-learn-stuff-quickly"><a href="https://www.joshwcomeau.com/blog/how-to-learn-stuff-quickly/">How To Learn Stuff Quickly</a></h2>
<p>It is easy to get stuck either in tutorial hell or reinvent every wheel when learning new things. <strong>Josh Comeau</strong> shares a few tips on how he has been able to pick up new skills quickly.</p>
<p><quote quote="The way I see it, skills are like wealth. The more skills I pick up, the faster they accumulate. Ideas and techniques gleaned in one domain can help in another" author="Josh Comeau"></quote></p>
<h3 id="key-takeaways-2">Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li>In the beginning, focus on guided learning. Once we are comfortable with the fundamentals, we should spend more time building interesting things and seeing tutorials only when encountering new problems.</li>
<li>A tutorial fade is effective, but we are building the same things over and over. Basically, after following a tutorial, you try to repeat it without looking at the tutorial.</li>
<li>We can add additional functionality to the tutorial project.</li>
<li>We can build a similar project to the tutorial project.</li>
<li>By publishing what we learn, we can uncover gaps in our mental model.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://www.joshwcomeau.com/blog/how-to-learn-stuff-quickly/">Link to Article</a></p>
<h2 id="when-it-pays-to-choose-microservices"><a href="https://blog.typeable.io/posts/2021-07-19-microservices.html">When it Pays to Choose Microservices</a></h2>
<p>Microservices are extremely popular, with a lot of big companies adopting them. But when is it worth switching over to microservices? <strong>Victoriia Zaripova</strong> outlines the pros and cons of developing a microservice-based solution.</p>
<p><quote quote="Like any other fancy solution, microservices aren't always beneficial. Neither do they give a plaster for all sores" author="Victoriia Zaripova"></quote></p>
<h3 id="key-takeaways-3">Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li>If microservices don't solve the business tasks, this is a waste of time and money.</li>
<li>Don't use microservices for a new solution.</li>
<li>The minimum pool for one microservice is a team of six to nine persons. One team should support a maximum of two microservices.</li>
<li>Consider using a microservice when
<ul>
<li>there are critical requirements for the application's resistance to loads and/or support of integration with external services</li>
<li>the business requires significant acceleration of development.</li>
<li>it's necessary to use a heterogeneous technology stack.</li>
<li>different points of the application require different rates of changes in various points of the application.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://blog.typeable.io/posts/2021-07-19-microservices.html">Link to Article</a></p>
<h2 id="the-nine-states-of-design"><a href="https://medium.com/swlh/the-nine-states-of-design-5bfe9b3d6d85">The Nine States of Design</a></h2>
<p>When designing UI, we tend to focus on the ideal state. <strong>Vince Speelman</strong> shares his list of the 9 different states that we should plan for to create a better experience for users who hit the unhappy path.</p>
<p><quote quote="As we learn to craft systems rather than pages, we must invest effort into shaping these often missed states of design and create with a component lifecycle that can support everyone" author="Vince Speelman"></quote></p>
<h3 id="key-takeaways-4">Key Takeaways</h3>
<ul>
<li>The different states that we should consider when building UI
<ul>
<li>Nothing state</li>
<li>Loading</li>
<li>None</li>
<li>One</li>
<li>Some</li>
<li>Too many</li>
<li>Incorrect</li>
<li>Correct</li>
<li>Done</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://medium.com/swlh/the-nine-states-of-design-5bfe9b3d6d85">Link to Article</a></p>
