<html><head></head><body><p>Every week I come across some great articles. Here are some of the posts I feel you will enjoy reading as much as I did.</p>
<p>Conversational copy is powerful, but it is hard to get right. <strong>Harry Dry</strong> has a <a href="https://marketingexamples.com/copywriting/conversational/">guide to conversational copy</a> that can help.</p>
<p>The conventional advice encourages everyone to become a manager. <strong>Charity Majors</strong> highlights the [<a href="https://charity.wtf/2019/09/08/reasons-not-to-be-a-manager/">17 reasons why you wouldn't want to become a manager</a> even in a healthy organization.</p>
<p>Building a good culture is hard. In this post, <strong>Mike Crittenden</strong> talks about what I think is the biggest mistake organizations make. Basically, r<a href="https://critter.blog/2020/10/15/the-hero-culture-conundrum/">eward the people who don't live the culture even though it looks like the latter directly benefits the business</a>.</p>
<p>Can you write code just by using your voice and eye-tracking? <strong>Josh Comeau</strong> shows his workflow for <a href="https://joshwcomeau.com/accessibility/hands-free-coding/">writing code using a microphone and an eye-tracker</a>.</p>
<p>It's easy for us to misunderstand harmless questions and statements. Paraphrasing can help us avoid this. <strong>Padmini Pyapali</strong> shares some <a href="https://smallbigideas.substack.com/p/no-more-misunderstandings">examples of how paraphrasing can be used in different contexts</a> to make a conversation more positive and productive. Although, I am not sure how achievable it is in practice.</p>
<p>Even though low code/no code won't replace programmers, it is the future because it makes technology more accessible to everyone. <strong>Rex Woodbury</strong> takes a look at the <a href="https://digitalnative.substack.com/p/roblox-airtable-and-the-building">no-code movement and the rise of creator platforms like Roblox</a>.</p>
<p>In case you missed it, I wrote a book summary of <a href="https://www.discoveriesinbookland.com/blog/death-meeting">Death by Meeting</a> by Patrick M. Lencioni.</p>
<p>Hope you enjoy reading these articles as much as I did.</p>
