<html><head></head><body><h2 id="what-is-speechcraft">What is Speechcraft</h2>
<p>Speechcraft is an introductory public speaking course spread over 7 weeks. Each week you have to write a 5-minute speech to meet a particular goal. Each goal builds on the next, so you end up stacking your learned skills.</p>
<h2 id="why-did-i-join-speechcraft">Why did I join Speechcraft</h2>
<p>To be honest, there was no real reason for me to join Speechcraft. I am not in a leadership position, nor do I have any public speaking engagements. But, my learning budget was going to waste, and I could use it towards Speechcraft, so I thought, why not.</p>
<h2 id="the-1st-day">The 1st day</h2>
<p>Since none of us was told to prepare any speeches, the 1st day was a little different.</p>
<p>First, the chairperson introduced us to the way Speechcraft works.</p>
<p>The agenda is roughly -</p>
<p>Everyone in the group gives a 5-minute speech on any subject of their choosing, which attempts to meet that week's goals.</p>
<ul>
<li>After all the speeches are done, the speeches are evaluated, and you get positive and negative feedback.</li>
<li>Then, there is a fun improv game called "Table Topics". A Table Topics Master is selected the prior week to come up with random topics for each member. Each member needs to speak to the given topic for a minute.</li>
</ul>
<p>Each of us was then asked what we hoped to get out of Speechcraft. It was interesting to see the different motivations. Some wanted to slow down their speaking; others wanted to become more concise. My reasons were simple.</p>
<ul>
<li>Learn how to use my body and voice while giving speeches.</li>
<li>Become more comfortable with impromptu speaking</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="whats-next">What's next?</h2>
<p>In the next few posts, I will talk about my speechcraft journey. I will be posting my intended speech, a video of what I actually spoke and an analysis. I might also add some feedback that is given to me by toastmasters.</p>
