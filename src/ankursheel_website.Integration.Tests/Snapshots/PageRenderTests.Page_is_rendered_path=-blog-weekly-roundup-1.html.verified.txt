<html><head></head><body><p>Every week I come across some great articles. Here are some of the posts I feel you will enjoy reading as much as I did.</p>
<p><strong>Graham</strong> from <strong>Reverse The Crush</strong> talks about why it is better to earn less, but do fulfilling work, than it is to make a lot, at the expense of a soul-crushing day job and why <a href="http://www.reversethecrush.com/%F0%9F%92%AB%E2%9C%8D%F0%9F%8F%BB%F0%9F%92%B0%F0%9F%92%BB%F0%9F%9B%A0%F0%9F%8F%9D-money-is-just-a-tool/">Money is just a tool</a>.</p>
<p>Budgets are essential, but how do you start? <strong>Allan</strong> from <strong>The Practical Saver</strong> recommends <a href="http://www.thepracticalsaver.com/personal-budget-categories-start-budget/">Personal budget categories</a> to help you get started.</p>
<p><strong>Kristin</strong> writes about some of the <a href="http://twocents.lifehacker.com/good-money-habits-that-can-hurt-your-credit-1785962981">intelligent financial moves aren't always the best credit moves</a> at <strong>Two Cents</strong>. Although aimed at the US market, the article gives a good insight into why credit cards are not necessarily a bad thing.</p>
<p><strong>Caz</strong> from <strong>yTravelBlog</strong> has a sponsored post on how to take a Luxury holiday for less by using the <a href="https://www.travelex.com/travelex-hub/travel-inspiration/luxury-for-less">Travelex Luxury for Less</a> aggregator.</p>
<p>Hope you enjoy reading these articles as much as I did.</p>
