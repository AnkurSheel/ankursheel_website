<html><head></head><body><p>Every week I come across some great articles. Here are some of the posts I feel you will enjoy reading as much as I did.</p>
<p><del>The build-vs-buy debate is interesting, and most tech companies will err on the side of build. <strong>Nočnica Fee</strong> explains the short-sightedness of this decision by way of a story of how a company goes from "make money" to "run servers"</del>.</p>
<p>I have never really enjoyed the daily standup because it gives me no useful information and feels like a status update. The team from <strong>LinearB</strong> shares the <a href="https://dev.to/linearb_inc/stand-up-2-0-it-s-time-to-ditch-the-daily-from-1993-5d6l">3 questions that we can ask to make this meeting more valuable</a>.</p>
<p>How do you spread the word about your product when you are just starting? <strong>Gabor Cselle</strong> gives <a href="https://medium.com/gabor/9-ways-to-build-virality-into-your-product-5975e1fe74e3">9 tips to add virality into your product</a>.</p>
<p>Everyone knows about ToDo lists. <strong>Drew Bredvick</strong> explains <a href="https://drew.tech/what-not-to-work-on">why Not-ToDo lists are as important when deciding what to work on</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Hiten Shah</strong> looks at some of the <a href="https://usefyi.com/atlassian-history/">strategies Atlassian employed to become a $10 Billion company</a>. The most interesting part of the case study is that they were self-funded and profitable before working with investors.</p>
<p><strong>Ramit Sethi</strong> shows you how to know which <a href="https://growthlab.com/people-who-never-buy/">type of customers won't buy from you</a> so that you concentrate on the ones that will.</p>
<p>Hope you enjoy reading these articles as much as I did.</p>
