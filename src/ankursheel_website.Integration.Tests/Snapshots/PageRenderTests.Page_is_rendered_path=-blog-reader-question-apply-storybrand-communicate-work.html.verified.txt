<html><head></head><body><h2 id="reader-question">Reader Question</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>Even though the Building a Storybrand seems to be mostly oriented towards marketing messaging, how would you apply it to general messaging at work?</p>
</blockquote>
<p><em>If you are wondering what post is being referred to its <a href="https://www.discoveriesinbookland.com/blog/building-a-storybrand">Building a Storybrand</a></em></p>
<h2 id="my-answer">My Answer</h2>
<p>I think the book's central premise around clear messages that do not require too much thinking is relevant, no matter the purpose of the communication.</p>
<p>If we are talking about it in a work context, there are 2 different perspectives.</p>
<h3 id="from-the-businesses-perspective">From the businesses perspective</h3>
<p>From a business perspective, I would say that a business needs to make sure that people can answer these questions without thinking.</p>
<ol>
<li>What is expected of me in my current role</li>
<li>What do I need to do to grow here?</li>
<li>What impact do I have on the business as an individual in my current role?</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="from-the-individuals-perspective">From the individual's perspective</h3>
<p>But what is more interesting is what can we as an individual do when communicating with colleagues.</p>
<p>We can use this framework to make sure that</p>
<ol>
<li>We are clear/consistent in our interactions with others so that they know what can be expected from us</li>
<li>We are clearly articulating the value we bring to the table for both the business and to other individuals</li>
</ol>
