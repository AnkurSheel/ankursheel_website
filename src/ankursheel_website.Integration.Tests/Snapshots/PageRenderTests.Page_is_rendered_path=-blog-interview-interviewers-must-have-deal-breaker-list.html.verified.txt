    <h1 class="text-center">Interviewing interviewers: Must-haves and deal-breakers list</h1>
    <div class="flex flex-col min-h-screen">
        <article>
            

<div class="prose-sm italic">
    <div class="flex flex-col sm:flex-row justify-evenly items-center text-sm gap-y-1">
        <span class="mr-4 sm:py-0 py-1">Published on <time
                datetime="2022-01-26">Jan 26, 2022</time></span>
        <span class="mr-4 sm:py-0 py-1">Last Updated on <time
                datetime="2022-10-06">Oct 06, 2022</time></span>
        <span class="sm:py-0 py-1">
            Estimated Reading Time: <span class="font-bold">6 min</span>
        </span>
    </div>

    <div class="flex flex-row flex-wrap gap-1 place-content-center">
            <a href="/tags/career" class="block flex-initial border-2 border-black m-2 rounded-xl p-2 bg-gray-200 shadow-md">career</a>
    </div>
</div>

<p>At the end of most interviews will come the question, &quot;Do you have any questions for us?&quot; Standard advice over the interwebs is that you should always have questions to ask because it will make you look more prepared and leave a good impression. But, there is a much more critical reason to interview your interviewer.</p>
<p>I have worked for a few companies in my career. Some of them worked out great, while for others, after a few months of joining, I realized it wasn't a great fit.</p>
<p>Joining a company and finding out it isn't a good match never feels great. It can be hard to know if the company we are considering joining is a good fit (before actually joining them). But, we can increase the chances by doing a little bit of introspection and preparation.</p>
<h2 id="how-to-apply-the-lists-in-an-interview">How to apply the lists in an interview</h2>
<p>Now, it's unlikely that we will be able to walk into a job interview and run through our list of must-haves/deal-breakers like a checklist. After all, we can't just ask our prospective boss if they are a micromanager. But we can look for signals that can help us evaluate them.</p>
<p>At this point, you might wonder, how do I do that?</p>
<ol>
<li>Make your own list of must-haves and deal-breakers.</li>
<li>For each item in the list, write a question.</li>
</ol>
<p><em>It's essential to keep the questions conversational and not accusatory. We should also structure the question, so the interviewer has to provide examples.</em></p>
<p>Keep the list of questions handy, so you can ask questions throughout the interview as they relate to the topic being discussed.</p>
<h2 id="my-must-have-list">My Must-Have List</h2>
<ul>
<li>A high degree of autonomy and flexibility in managing my time and workload.</li>
<li>Open communication among teammates and with management and Human Resources.</li>
<li>Ability to choose my workplace tools, including both hardware and software.</li>
<li>A clear and defined path toward career advancement (both in skills and compensation)</li>
<li>Ability to work on non-work-related tasks for a few hours a week.</li>
<li>Opportunity to spend time sharing my ideas on either mine or a company blog.</li>
<li>Learning from mistakes / Blameless</li>
<li>Inclusive Work environment</li>
<li>Equality when it comes to stock options, reviews etc</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="my-deal-breaker-list">My Deal Breaker List</h2>
<ul>
<li>Expected overtime.</li>
<li>T-shirts/ Shorts and Sandals are not allowed.</li>
<li>Mandatory in-office days/ No remote working.</li>
<li>If completely remote, there is no office space or compensation to pay for a shared office space.</li>
<li>Mandatory after-hours on-call.</li>
<li>Micro-management.</li>
<li>Lots of meetings.</li>
<li>Following Dogmatic Scrum and story pointing.</li>
<li>Long-lived branches.</li>
<li>No automated tests / over-reliance on manual QA.</li>
<li>No QA / over-reliance on automated tests / only developers testing</li>
<li>Hiring mainly seniors / Not many intermediates or juniors.</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Note that these items are all about me, my values and what I want. They have very little to do with any particular job or workplace.</em></p>
<h2 id="turning-the-list-into-questions">Turning the list into questions</h2>
<p>Now that I have my must-have and deal-breaker lists, it's time to turn them into questions. There might be overlapping questions, so it's best to combine them.</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Can you walk me through your development process, from a ticket or task to code on production?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Autonomy and Flexibility, Dogmatic Scrum, Lots of meetings</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How do you decide what products people work on? How much say do engineers have over what is built and how it's built?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Autonomy and Flexibility</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Do you encourage public discussions, especially for controversial topics and disagreements?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Open Communication</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How are disagreements solved - technical disputes and other kinds? What happens when personalities clash?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Open Communication</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How often does the leadership team discuss turnover percentages and other numbers</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Open Communication</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Can programmers use their preferred OS, code editor, and other tools?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Workplace tools</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Do you have a career ladder and skill matrix with parallel IC and management tracks?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Career Advancement</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Do you have a shared compensation matrix?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Career Advancement</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What percentage of people have been promoted internally to leadership positions?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Career Advancement</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What are further opportunities for this role? What is the next career level?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Career Advancement</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How do you give back to the community? Open Source/ Blog Articles etc.? Is it encouraged?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>:  Ability to work on non-work-related tasks, Share Ideas</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Do you have regular retros? Process/Team and after features?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>:  Learning from mistakes</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What does the company do to ensure an inclusive work environment for transgender people, people with disabilities, single parents, and other under-represented folks? How is diversity represented within the team or company? Does HR get input for policies or people programs?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>:  Inclusiveness</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How responsive are people to emails/Slack over the weekends during the day and after 6pm?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Overtime</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What is the on-call schedule?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Mandatory on call</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How often are there emergencies or times when people have to work extra hours?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Mandatory on-call, Expected overtime</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Are people compensated for being on call? How do you measure the impact on developers?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Mandatory on call</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How often are engineers expected to provide status updates on a project?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Autonomy and Flexibility, Micro-management</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Who sets deadlines, and what happens when people fail to meet them?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Autonomy and Flexibility, Micro-management</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How often do you have meetings? Are there any scheduled/standing meetings?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Lots of status meetings</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How does this team plan projects and create estimates?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Autonomy and Flexibility, Dogmatic Scrum</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How often is the product released?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Long-lived branches</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What is the median age for which an issue and a subsequent PR will be open?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Long-lived branches</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What does your QA process look like?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: No automated tests</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: What is the ratio of junior/intermediate/senior engineers?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Hiring mainly seniors</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How do you ensure that junior engineers are supported?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Hiring mainly seniors, Learning from mistakes</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Does everyone get equity allocation or a part of profit-sharing?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Equality</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Can you give me an example of a mistake you've made here and how it was handled?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Open Communication, Learning from mistakes</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Has there been a time when the engineering team was on the wrong course?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Open Communication, Learning from mistakes</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: Does the company collect workplace feedback? Have you ever acted on it?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Learning from mistakes</p>
<p><strong>Q</strong>: How do performance reviews work? Do engineers review their peers and managers? What do you do in case of a bad review of a manager?</p>
<p><em>Relates to</em>: Equality, Career Advancement</p>
<h2 id="what-is-an-appropriate-number-of-questions-to-ask">What is an appropriate number of questions to ask?</h2>
<p>This is quite an extensive list. We won't be able to ask all of them in a single interview. Most interviews keep only the last couple of minutes for questions. However, when interviewing for jobs, there are often multiple rounds. I would suggest choosing questions to ask at the different stages. After all, some questions are better answered by a hiring manager, while others are better answered by someone working in a similar role.</p>
<p>One thing I have not tried so far but intend to try next time I am interviewing is conducting a <a href="https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/reverse-interviewing/" target="_blank">reverse interview</a> after I have negotiated the offer to get any unanswered questions answered.</p>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>A list of questions based on our must-haves and deal-breakers can give us the confidence to know that we are making the right decision. We can down positions or step away from the interview process where there isn't a great fit. You owe it to yourself to find a place aligned with your values.</p>
