<html><head></head><body><p>Every week I come across some great articles. Here are some of the posts I feel you will enjoy reading as much as I did.</p>
<p>There are so <a href="https://www.davidsheff.com/jeff-bezos">many little gems in this 1999 interview of Jeff Bezos</a> on <strong>David Sheff</strong>'s blog.</p>
<p>What is the difference between the Severity and Priority of bugs? Even though they are different, you can have different combinations, making it harder to decide which bug to fix. <strong>Dan Emmons</strong> <a href="https://medium.com/@dan.ryan.emmons/qa-severity-and-priority-guide-7bacf316db49">explains the different combinations in an obvious way</a>.</p>
<p>Data seeding is critical for achieving isolated test runs. <strong>Viktor Slavchev</strong> walks us through the <a href="https://mrslavchev.com/2020/04/01/hindsight-lessons-about-automation-the-mastery-of-data-seeding/">multiple ways of setting up data for automated checks</a>.</p>
<p>There are a lot of articles out there that say that we should take a mobile-first approach for our site or app. <strong>Matt Anderson</strong> shows a <a href="https://mattanddesign.com/dont-design-for-mobile/">few examples of where this categorisation starts to break and why we should look at device capabilities instead</a>.</p>
<p>Even if people know what the right thing is, people will always choose the more convenient thing. <strong>Jason Lengstorf</strong> explains why we don't need to convince others that something is the right thing to do. We just need to <a href="https://lengstorf.com/right-thing-easy-thing/">make it easier to do the thing we want to be done than to do anything else</a>.</p>
<p>Statistics are unhelpful without context. <strong>Robert Heaton</strong> <a href="https://robertheaton.com/2019/02/24/making-peace-with-simpsons-paradox/">dissolves some common examples of Simpson's paradox with better framing</a>.</p>
<p>Hope you enjoy reading these articles as much as I did.</p>
